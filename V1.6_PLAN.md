# v1.6 — "Depth danger" (design plan, no code yet)

**Origin:** playtest of a full 5-floor descent (dwarf barbarian, 14 HP / AC 12) took **2 HP** total damage,
never needed a bandage, and ended with a surplus of consumables. The engine and content work; the crawl
has no teeth. This plan gives deep floors teeth **without breaking a single existing golden transcript.**

Status: **design only.** Nothing here is implemented. Numbers are proposals to be tuned against playtest + gate.

---

## 1. Confirmed diagnosis (grounded in the code)

| Claim | Verdict | Detail |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Monster damage trivial | **Mostly right, one correction** | `rollDamage` ([combat.zig:80](src/combat.zig:80)) = `1d6 + STR mod`, `@max(·,0)`. Per *hit* that's **0–5** (goblin, STR 8 → −1) / **1–6** (skeleton, STR 10 → 0), avg ~2.5 / 3.5 — **not** "0–1". The `@max(sum,0)` only zeroes a goblin natural-1. |
| Real trivializer | **Accuracy + short fights + heals** | `attackModifier` ([combat.zig:74](src/combat.zig:74)) is *only* the STR mod (no proficiency). Goblin needs d20 ≥ 13 (**40%**), skeleton ≥ 12 (**45%**) vs AC 12. A barbarian one/two-shots a 7-HP goblin, so each fight is 1–3 monster turns → ~1–4 total damage, fully absorbed by 14 HP + bandages. |
| Depth adds quantity not lethality | **Confirmed** | `depthTier` drives extra *spawns* ([dungeon.zig:293](src/dungeon.zig:293)) and up to `tier×4` extra *loot* attempts ([dungeon.zig:347](src/dungeon.zig:347)). Per-monster HP/AC/damage/attack are fixed `block()` constants ([monsters.zig:19](src/monsters.zig:19)) and never see `floor_index`. Deeper = more weak enemies **and** more heals. |
| `block.damage_die` is dead | **No** | `spawnMonster` copies it into `ent.damage_die` ([world.zig:244](src/world.zig:244)); `weaponDamageDie` returns it ([inventory.zig:162](src/inventory.zig:162)). The die is live — the **modifiers** are the problem. |

**Takeaway:** the fix is not "bigger dice." It is (a) give deep monsters a real attack + damage + HP + AC edge,
(b) add tougher elite kinds, and (c) stop drowning the player in heals — **all keyed to depth.**

## 2. The load-bearing constraint: the frozen `reference_crawl`

`references/reference_crawl.txt` is the committed golden the release gate checks, pinned to `# version=1.1.0`
(regenerated by [build.zig:327](build.zig:327), guarded by `version.isFrozenReference`). It runs floors **1→3**
and its transcript contains **live combat numbers and loot listings**:

- Line 29: `attack goblin_0->entity_0 roll=16 mod=-1 vs AC 12 hit damage=1 hp=12/13` — a floor-2 goblin hit.
- Lines 67–74: a floor-3 goblin fight (`mod=-1`, `vs AC 15`, `damage=10/13`).
- Lines 88–96: a floor-3 `look` listing every loot item on that floor.

And `depthTier(3) = 1`, so **floor 3 already consumes the depth-bonus RNG** and already spawns bonus monsters/loot.

> **Any lethality or loot change that reaches floors ≤ 3 alters this frozen golden** (plus `brawl`,
> `crawl_start`, `save_roundtrip`, `playthrough`, `descend_crawl`, `geared_brawl`, `corpse_loot`,
> `deep_floor`'s floor-2 baseline, etc.). Per the determinism contract that is a deliberate, version-gated act —
> not something to do incidentally.

### Design decision (recommended): gate ALL new lethality to `floor_index ≥ 4`

Introduce a **new** `dangerTier(floor_index) = 0 for floor < 4, else min(floor_index - 3, CAP)`.
Leave the existing `depthTier` (count scaling) **exactly as-is** so floor-1–3 spawn/loot counts and the
floor-3 bonus RNG stream never move. Then:

- Floors 1–3 (everything `reference_crawl` and the legacy goldens touch) stay **byte-for-byte identical.**
- Only floors 4+ change — and nothing before v1.6 has a golden that descends that deep, so **the only new
  goldens are the v1.6 scenarios.**

This also reads as intentional difficulty design: floors 1–3 are the warm-up ramp; the teeth start where the
finding said the crawl went slack. The min-1 damage floor and depth bonuses attach only to danger-tier
monsters, so floors 1–3 are untouched by them too.

> **Alternative (Option B, not recommended unless you want early floors to bite):** re-freeze
> `reference_crawl` at `1.6.0`, rebalance floors 1–3 as well, and apply the min-1 damage floor globally.
> Heavier: it reopens a deliberately frozen artifact, regenerates the reference golden, and updates
> `version.isFrozenReference`/[build.zig:327](build.zig:327). Keep Option A unless there's a reason.

## 3. Track 1 — Depth-scaled base monster stats (floor ≥ 4)

Attach a single per-instance `danger_tier` (0 on floors 1–3) to each spawned monster; all bonuses derive from it.

| Stat | Change | Effect (proposed) |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| Attack | `+dangerTier` on the attack roll (on top of STR mod) | Floor-4 goblin −1 → 0; floor-6 → +2. Hit chance 40% → ~55–65% vs AC 12. |
| Damage | `1d6 + STR + dangerTier`, **min 1 on a hit** | Floor-6 goblin avg ~5.5/hit; a hit always costs ≥ 1. Min applies **only when `danger_tier > 0`**. |
| Max HP | `max_hp + dangerTier * K` (K ≈ 3) | Longer fights → more monster turns → more accumulated damage. |
| AC | `+ (dangerTier / 2)` | Player occasionally misses, extending fights. |

**Where it lives**
- Compute `danger_tier` in `planMonsterSpawns` (it already has `floor_index`); carry it on `MonsterSpawn`.
- `spawnMonster` applies HP immediately (HP round-trips free — `max_hp`/`current_hp` are already persisted).
- `combat.zig` reads the entity's `danger_tier`: `attackModifier` adds `+danger_tier`; `rollDamage` adds
  `+danger_tier` then `@max(1, dmg)` when `danger_tier > 0`; `targetAc` adds `+ danger_tier/2`.

**Persistence** — AC and attack bonus are recomputed at read time (AC from kind, attack from STR), so they do
**not** survive save/load today. Persist the source of truth:
- Add `danger_tier: u32 = 0` to `EntitySave` ([save_state.zig:20](src/save_state.zig:20)).
- Bump `schema_version` **3 → 4**; add a v3→v4 migration that defaults `danger_tier = 0` (old saves = no danger).
- New DST `save_v4_roundtrip` (see §6). This satisfies the ROADMAP rule "when `schema_version` bumps, add a
  migration and a roundtrip DST scenario."

## 4. Track 2 — Elite variants (floor ≥ 4)

Add mundane elite kinds to `monsters.Kind` + `block()` (no magic — skeletons already ship as animated dead):

| Kind | Family | Proposed block | Corpse |
|------|--------|----------------|--------|
| `hobgoblin` | goblin | STR 13, HP 16, AC 16, d6 | `short_sword` (as goblin) |
| `skeleton_warrior` | skeleton | STR 13, HP 20, AC 15, d8 | none yet / `short_sword` (bones sink is backlog) |
| `ogre` *(optional, floor 6, rare)* | — | STR 16, HP 30, AC 13, d10 | `club` |

- Spawn elites **only in the `floor_index ≥ 4` branch** so the existing tier-1 (floor-3) bonus RNG consumption
  is untouched — this keeps `reference_crawl` byte-identical. Draw the elite upgrade from a stream/branch that
  only executes when `dangerTier > 0`.
- Elites still carry `danger_tier` on top, so a floor-6 hobgoblin is nastier than a floor-4 one.
- Plumbing: extend `combat.monsterKind` ([combat.zig:52](src/combat.zig:52)) to match the new names/`char_name`s;
  extend `monsterName` ([dungeon.zig:240](src/dungeon.zig:240)); add corpse drops in the kill path. Elites
  round-trip via stored `name`/`char_name` + stats + `danger_tier` — no schema change beyond §3's field.

## 5. Track 3 — Cut the consumable glut (floor ≥ 4)

Floors 1–3 loot stays frozen (`reference_crawl` lists it). On danger floors only:

- Replace the `tier * 4` bonus-loot cap ([dungeon.zig:351](src/dungeon.zig:351)) with a small cap
  (≈ `dangerTier` attempts) for floor ≥ 4.
- Bias the deep-floor pick table **away from `bandage`** (e.g. deep bonus loot draws from
  `{antidote, rations, leather_armour}`, bandage only ~1-in-6).
- Net: deeper = fewer heals while monsters hit harder → the survival system finally creates pressure.

## 8. Tuning knobs (starting values, expect to iterate)

| Knob | Proposed default | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| `dangerTier` cap | 3 (floors 4,5,6+) | Matches the 5-floor product scope; floor 6 = tier 3. |
| Attack bonus | `+dangerTier` | Cheapest, highest-leverage lever — accuracy is the trivializer. |
| Damage bonus | `+dangerTier`, min 1 | Guarantees hits cost something. |
| HP multiplier `K` | 3 | Lengthens fights → more monster turns land. |
| AC bonus | `dangerTier / 2` | Keep modest; player misses shouldn't feel unfair. |
| Deep bandage rarity | ~1-in-6 of deep bonus loot | Primary scarcity lever. |
